I am building a personal website that will contain links to my projects.

### 1. The "Stage" (The Room)

* **Non-Scrollable:** The entire website fits within the user's screen (100% height/width). It feels like a fixed "view" or a photograph rather than a scrolling document.
* **The Cupboard:** This is the primary structural element. It is a large, wooden-framed piece of furniture recessed into a wall.
* **The Shelf:** Inside the cupboard is a single, deep shelf. We use "1-point perspective" with vanishing point above the stack and to the right of the boxes to show the depth of the interior walls (top, bottom, and sides of the shelf cavity). The shelf is the central element on the screen with it's borders visible at the top and the bottom of the page and it's sides creating boundaries for the main content of the page.
* **Content outside of the shelf** To the left of the shelf it's just clear background representing the wall. To the right it's "inside" of the cupboard that for now will be just a background of a different colour but we may consider adding e.g. clothes hanging or something similar purely as a background visual - no interactions required.

### 2. The Shoeboxes (The Projects)

* **Physical Stacking:** Instead of a neat digital grid, the boxes are stacked "imperfectly" on top of each other, starting from the floor of the shelf.
* **Anatomical Realism:** Each box is a 3D object with a **Front** (facing the viewer), a **Top** (the lid), and a **Side**.
* **Variable Dimensions:** To avoid a "copy-paste" look, we are using real-world measurements (11 x 20.5 x 33 and 12.5 x 23 x 36) to give the boxes different heights and widths.
* **The "Off-Center" View:** We are looking at the stack from a slightly low-left angle, meaning we see the **top surface** and the **right side** of the boxes.

### 3. The Details

* **Masking Tape Labels:** Project titles aren't just text on the box; they are written on strips of "masking tape" or "stickers". These have jagged, ripped edges and are stuck on at slightly crooked angles.
* **Tactile Interaction:** When you hover over a box, it should "slide" forward out ("pull-out" motion) of the cupboard slightly, making it feel like you are physically selecting it from a collection.
* **Curation Space:** Because the boxes are offset to one side, the empty space on the shelf remains for "environmental" storytelling like some stickers or objects at the back of the shelf that we can add later, e.g. maybe with the link to "about me" page.
* **Colour Palette** We will use pastel colours with shoeboxes have distinct colours that a close to the theme of each project to add more more distinction on top of the labels.

### 4. The Mobile Transformation

* **The Front-Facing Flip:** We rotate the boxes so the user sees them from the "Short Side" (the front) to fill out the space on the screen effectively.
* **Shelf size** As the mobile (vertical) viewport is very narrow we are going to make sure that the shelf is effectively becomes the view point with each sides of the shelf "sticking" to the sides of the screen.
